Business interpretation

Process-level opportunity

Only 30.85% of candidates who take the technical test receive an offer. The same pattern is observed across all roles, indicating a broader process opportunity.

  • Review assessment calibration
  • Improve qualification before testing
  • Standardize offer decision criteria
  • Strengthen hiring manager calibration

Interpretation

Volume alone does not explain conversion

Gustavo managed the largest pipeline, with 121 candidates, but achieved one of the lowest hire conversion rates at 5.0%. Bruno and Ana handled smaller pipelines and reached the highest conversion rates, at 15.3% and 13.3%.

These differences may reflect role complexity, seniority, source mix, or requisition difficulty. Their practices could be reviewed to identify transferable sourcing and qualification approaches.

Recruiter performance should be interpreted together with portfolio complexity. However, the gap between Bruno and Ana versus the remaining recruiters indicates an opportunity to review and share practices across the team.

Observed pattern

Hired candidates combine multiple positive signals

Hired candidates have, on average, more professional experience and stronger technical, behavioral, and manager assessment scores than non-hired candidates.

Important limitation

Scores should not be used as early-stage predictors

Assessment scores appear in records for candidates who did not reach the corresponding stages. This indicates potential data leakage and limits their use in predictive or automated decision models.

When to persist

Response speed is not a linear predictor of hiring

Candidates who took 6–7 days to respond presented the highest hire rate in the dataset, at 14.9%. Candidates who replied after 11 days also converted at 11.5%.

This suggests that recruiters should not automatically deprioritize or close candidates solely because of delayed responses, especially when other signals indicate potential alignment with the role.

Persistence decisions should combine response behavior with profile quality, assessment signals, role fit, and candidate context. Response time alone is not a reliable stop signal.

Executive Conclusion

The consolidated interpretation of the analysis.

The recruitment process demonstrates consistent performance throughout the early stages of the hiring funnel.

The largest opportunity lies in improving the Technical Test → Offer transition, a pattern consistently observed across every analyzed role.

Candidate engagement also represents an important opportunity, as No Response accounts for the largest share of documented rejections.

Finally, improving data quality will increase analytical reliability and enable safer, more advanced AI-supported recruiting workflows.
